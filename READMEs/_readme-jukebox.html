JUKEBOX 2019.1

This AddOn allows you to add an any amount of background music to your game. You can also specify where and when the music shall play.

  • Add a menu music to your game that plays while not logged in (= in the char creation/selection screen / this only works in a compiled client).
  • Add a default music that plays while logged in when no other music plays.
  • Add a battle music to your game that plays while engaged in combat.
  • Add any amount of music areas to your game that change the current song when entering and revert back to the default song when leaving.
  • Music areas provide several options like loop, if they take precedence over battle music or not and so on.
  • When a music area takes precedence, the music will change even if the battle music currently plays. This is good for boss areas where you want a specific song to play all the time.

INSTALLATION PROCESS

Integration Steps

  1. [Required] In this AddOns PREFABS folder.
  2. [Required] Locate "UCE_Tmpl_Jukebox" scriptable object and edit however you like.
  3. [Required] Locate "NetworkManager" in your scene and assign "UCE_Tmpl_Jukebox" to it.
  4. [Optional] Select the "UCE_Area_Jukebox" prefab from this AddOns prefab folder and drag it somewhere into your scene. Adjust its settings in the inspector.
  5. [Optional] Try to walk into and out of such a music area when playing the game.
  6. [Optional] Try to attack or get attacked by an enemy.
  7. [Optional] Try to enter/exit a music area right after attacking or being attacked.
  8. [Optional] Set the "priority" checkmark to the JukeboxArea, play the game, get attacked and walk into the JukeboxArea immediately afterwards again.

Additional Information

  • Please note that the menu music is only functional in a compiled client.
  • A option menu to adjust music & sound volume will be added later.
  • Make sure that you only have one "SimpleJukeBox" Scriptable Object in your project (although multiple ones won't cause any errors). Should you delete this scriptable object by accident, you can always create a new one via right-mouse-click and "Create".
  • All audio used is freely available as open-source at http://www.opengameart.org
